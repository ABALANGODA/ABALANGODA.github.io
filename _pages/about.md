Welcome to my academic homepage! I am an Assistant Teaching Professor in the Department of Geology & Environmental Science at the University of Pittsburgh.

I am an Assistant Teaching Professor in the Department of Geology & Environmental Science at the University of Pittsburgh, where my research and teaching center on the health of aquatic ecosystems. My work examines nutrient cycling, harmful algal blooms, and eutrophication in lakes, reservoirs, and urban streams, with particular attention to how drinking water treatment practices influence downstream water quality and ecological function.

My teaching spans large introductory courses to advanced field- and lab-based electives, including Environmental Science, Ecosystem Ecology, Environmental Geochemistry, Limnology, and Statistics for Earth Science, as well as an undergraduate research capstone in environmental science. I have earned Associate, Practitioner, and Scholar-level teaching certificates through the University of Pittsburgh's evidence-based STEM teaching program, and my work in course design has been recognized through multiple db-SERC Course Transformation Awards and the Nancy Tannery Award for Open Educational Resources. I am committed to inquiry-based, hands-on learning, and I regularly mentor undergraduate and graduate students through independent research and capstone projects. Explore my Teaching page to learn more about the courses I teach, or visit my Publications and CV for further details on my research.
